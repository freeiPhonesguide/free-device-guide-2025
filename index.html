Understanding the Programs: Lifeline & ACP Changes for 2025

Two main programs have helped people get affordable communication services and devices:

  • Lifeline: A long-standing FCC program providing a monthly discount on phone (landline/wireless) or internet service. This discount often makes basic service and sometimes a basic phone free.
  • Affordable Connectivity Program (ACP): This program offered a larger discount on internet and a one-time discount for a device. IMPORTANT: As of early 2024, the ACP stopped accepting new applications due to lack of funding. For 2025, this means the ACP device discount is not available for new applicants. Lifeline continues, but device options are mainly through what Lifeline providers might offer with their service plans.

You can often qualify for Lifeline if you participate in programs like SNAP, Medicaid, SSI, Federal Public Housing Assistance, Veterans Pension, or Tribal programs.

Are You Eligible for Lifeline?

You can qualify for the Lifeline program in two main ways. Remember to check the most current 2025 guidelines on LifelineSupport.org as income levels are updated annually.

1. Based on Your Income

Your household income must be at or below 135% of the Federal Poverty Guidelines for your household size. These guidelines change, so always verify the current year's numbers.

2. Based on Program Participation

You or someone in your household participates in one of these federal assistance programs:

  • Supplemental Nutrition Assistance Program (SNAP)
  • Medicaid
  • Supplemental Security Income (SSI)
  • Federal Public Housing Assistance (FPHA)
  • Veterans Pension and Survivors Benefit
  • Tribal Specific Programs (e.g., Bureau of Indian Affairs General Assistance, Tribally-Administered TANF, Food Distribution Program on Indian Reservations, Head Start for Tribal lands)

What You'll Need: Required Documents

To apply, you'll need to provide official proof of your identity, address, and eligibility (either through income or program participation). Make clear digital copies (scans or photos) if applying online.

  • Valid Driver’s License or State ID

🏠 Proof of Address

Tips for a Smooth Process & Avoiding Scams

👍 Tips for Success

  • Accuracy is Key: Double-check all information on your application matches your documents exactly.
  • Clear Documents: Submit legible copies. Blurry or incomplete documents cause major delays.
  • Keep Copies: Save copies of your application and all submitted documents.
  • Be Patient, Then Proactive: Allow standard processing times. If significantly delayed, politely follow up.
  • Use Official Resources: Stick to LifelineSupport.org and official FCC guides.
  • Check 2025 Updates: Program rules and provider offerings can change.

⚠️ Scam Alerts: Protect Yourself!

  • No Upfront Fees for "Free" Devices: Lifeline is a service discount. Be wary of demands for payment for the "free" phone itself.
  • No Unsolicited Info Requests: Legitimate agencies won't cold call/text/email for SSN, bank details, or credit card info to qualify you.
  • Beware Unofficial Sites: Stick to .gov sites or reputable provider sites. Watch for typos or high-pressure tactics.
  • "Too Good To Be True" Offers: Lifeline usually provides basic, functional smartphones, not the latest high-end models for free.
  • Report Scams: Report suspicious activity to the FTC (ReportFraud.ftc.gov) and the FCC.

The Lifeline program itself is legitimate and has helped millions. Your awareness is your best defense.
